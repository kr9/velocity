Future data will be more personal than ever. A closer look at the impact of unauthorized data collection by corporations and government agencies.

![](../../assets/images/wp/civil-war-2-2.png)

Humans are approaching towards the universe where the most important asset is their data. People have a bipolar tendency. Either guard the property on their own or have professionals manage them. The belief plays a crucial role in deciding, but the transition from one to another is never easy. It is rather unsettling when it takes too long.

Conventionally people keep a guarded safe to store their valuables or rent a safe deposit box. While some of the principles overlap, digital data is vastly different from physical items.

It is easy to replicate, manipulate and gain insight out of data. Technology is evolving and people have to make choices. They are forced to accept the trade-offs among privacy, security, accessibility, productivity and affordability. In the end, a majority of the people do not get what they exactly want. They tend to carry away by illusionary truth effect.

## My service for your data

The irony to expensive product marketing is that protecting privacy is also costly. Users are considered a product and the personal data is being used to gain profits. It is questionable whether it will always be win-win for online advertising platforms and advertisers. In the end, the user has to pay the cost of time, efficiency, convenience or impulse buying. Instead of a fundamental right, privacy has a huge price tag. The complete system is evolving such a way that there is no escape for the user. Either use the services or go through the inconvenience beyond imagination. Products such as [Blackphone](https://www.silentcircle.com/products-and-solutions/devices/), [TrustedID](https://www.trustedid.com/), [Aegis Padlock](http://www.apricorn.com/products/hardware-encrypted-drives/padlock-ssd.html), [DataLocker Hard Drives](https://datalocker.com/product-category/encrypted-storage/harddrives/) or [Tarsnap](http://www.tarsnap.com/) cost a lot in comparison to free services. Moreover, even after doing everything, there is no guarantee that the data will stay completely private. It is exceptionally inconvenient to protect privacy. Users will feel out of sync from their personal and professional life.

## I don't have anything to hide

It is interesting to notice the evolving perspective of people about their data.

> Every single time somebody has said to me, "I don't really worry about invasions of privacy because I don't have anything to hide."
>
> [Glenn Greenwald](https://twitter.com/ggreenwald) in his TED Talk — [Why Privacy Matters](http://www.ted.com/talks/glenn_greenwald_why_privacy_matters)

> I always say the same thing to them. I get out a pen, I write down my email address. I say, "Here's my email address. What I want you to do when you get home is email me the passwords to all of your email accounts, not just the nice, respectable work one in your name, but all of them, because I want to be able to just troll through what it is you're doing online, read what I want to read and publish whatever I find interesting. After all, if you're not a bad person, if you're doing nothing wrong, you should have nothing to hide. Not a single person has taken me up on that offer."

No matter how open people would think themselves, they will not feel comfortable knowing most of their data and online actions are being watched and can be used against them in numerous forms. I would like to classify the evolving generation as "Generation C (Cloud)". They are fanatic about being minimal, social and accessible. They will soon figure out the cost of their data is more than just the free service.

## I am watching you

How would you feel when you realize that the online communication you had with your girlfriend had been used to market better parenting books to you? Many such examples surfaced in past few years. Online service providers screen user's data to serve falsely relevant ads in exchange for free services. They exploit every bit of personal information to gain profit. That is their business model.

> "This is the tragic flaw of 'privacy' in the digital age. Privacy is often defined as freedom from unauthorized intrusion. But many of the things that feel like privacy violations are 'authorized' in some fine print somewhere."
>
> — [Julia Angwin](https://twitter.com/JuliaAngwin), [Who is watching you?](https://medium.com/backchannel/who-is-watching-you-7296eeb036c1)

It uncovers compelling facts and instances which make you think carefully before dealing with any form of online data. In most cases, there aren't just intruders but the people and companies we trust watching us.

## What is at stake?

Above anything else, creativity, expression, and future of innovation are at stake. The very instinctive and subconscious nature of human beings causes originality to compromise when they realize that someone or something is watching them. The impact might not be visible for years or even decades. But when it surfaces, the long term damage is irreversible.

Government authorities are increasingly collecting data from service provider companies and even general web and voice traffic. They are doing all these in the disguise of national security and inter-country intelligence. One of the apparent examples is the way travel systems have become inefficient and frustrating. There is no guarantee that the data collected by these agencies will not be imperiled one day. Even worse, it might be used against the people who intend no harm to anyone and just want to enjoy their simple life.

It is becoming easier to undermine the weak encryption. The business of ransomware is booming. The data threats are going to increase in coming years. In general, people with malicious intent are far ahead of the rest when it comes to resources and use of technology. Instead of focusing on how to advance the technology to detect the real anomaly and make it ubiquitous, the general screening has lost focus and drifted away in the wrong direction.

## Conflict for data?

The volume and variety of data are growing faster than ever. It is getting increasingly intricate to manage it. For many of us, privacy is not the priority right now. It only takes precedence when something unanticipated crosses our path. Future data will be more personal than ever. The rise of big data technologies is making it easier to analyze a large amount of public data.

Conscious efforts and disciplinary data management strategies will be required. It will help diffuse the impact of human life manipulation by the use of personal data. As it is happening with global warming, the world might not be on the brink of possible conflict between authorities and individual data owners but, without a doubt, this possibility is being forced upon us.
